Submission Guidelines

Submissions to The Voice of Dog should:

  • be less than ~5000 words per episode (thus less than ~10,000 words total for a two-part submission.)
    • We can sometimes accept longer works, though an authorization from a reader who intends to cover that story is needed (this can be yourself, as authors are encouraged to, if they wish, read their own stories.)
    • We cannot accept works that would require more than two sequential episodes.
    • We may consider accepting an excerpt from a longer work, such as a chapter from a novel, provided that excerpt stands on its own as a complete narrative and doesn’t require the listener to know the rest of the story to make sense.
  • be furry, ie be:
    • about the furry community,
    • or by a furry author,
    • or contain furry or anthropomorphic animal characters,
    • or be relevant to furry or anthropomorphic issues or topics.
  • not advocate for or praise hateful or bigoted views, groups, or ideologies.
  • not be “A Bummer,” ie, while dark, sad, or grim stories are welcomed, they should contain elements of hope, should “end looking up” so to speak. Specific application of this guideline is up to the interpretation of the Voice of Dog, and is open for discussion with an interested author regarding a specific work.
  • be your own words: we cannot accept
    • stories you commissioned from another writer unless that writer is co-submitting the piece with you,
    • fanfiction involving characters not original to you (fanfiction settings for stories with original characters may be accepted at our discretion,)
    • or works containing text generated by an “AI” machine learning algorithm.

By submitting, you grant The Voice of Dog non-exclusive rights to record and distribute an audio reading, as a derivative work, with credit. In submitting a story you affirm that it is your own work and that you have the ability to grant these rights. You retain all rights to the original written work. We will raise no objection to any other form of publication or distribution of any kind. We accept previously published work, provided there is no rights conflict or exclusivity period with the previous publisher. You retain the right to withdraw your submission, at any time, for any reason, with written or electronic notice.

We do not offer monetary compensation, nor will we make firm commitments regarding when or how quickly a story will be read.

All of our readers are volunteers. We therefore do not offer direct creative control over the reading itself. You are encouraged to use the notes section of the submission template to make suggestions and give notes on tone and characters, and are encouraged to discuss and collaborate with a reader if they wish, but a finished reading shall be accepted as-is. Neither we nor submitting authors may require retakes or revisions.

We reserve the right to remove an episode from the archives at our discretion, should we feel it necessary.

If you have any questions or concerns, or would like to get the templates to submit a story, please contact us on Telegram, Bluesky, Mastodon, or by email.
